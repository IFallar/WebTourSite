BATANGAS

GENERAL INFORMATION

Enjoy a slice of paradise in the seaside towns and villages of Batangas. Dive into the depths of Anilao, Batangas’ underwater Eden. Swim and dive in the tropical waters of Verde Island Passage, which has been described as the world’s center of marine biodiversity. Play on Laiya Aplaya, San Juan’s famous stretch of white sand beach, and explore the numerous offshore islets. Taal Heritage Village transports you back in time with its well-preserved ancestral houses. Visit the many centuries-old churches, each of which is architecturally stunning. Try the local Batangas coffee and watch the Balisong-making process. Batangas, a multifaceted beauty, never ceases to captivate everyone with its many charms and wonderful surprises.

It is the Philippines' fourth richest and ninth most populous province. It is also the birthplace of Tagalog culture. Because of its proximity to Metro Manila, Batangas is a popular tourist destination in Southern Luzon, with a number of resorts and heritage sites. With its numerous Roman Catholic churches and pilgrimage sites, the province is also becoming a popular pilgrimage destination.

Anilao Diving

Explore the marine treasures of Anilao in Batangas, known as the Philippines’ nudibranch capital. Read this Anilao diving guide to learn how to get there, where to go diving, and what kind of critters to expect. The province of Batangas, part of the Calabarzon region, is located in the southwest corner of Luzon. It is well-known for tourist attractions such as the Taal Volcano, Taal Heritage Town (where you can find ancestral houses dating back to the 19th century), beaches, and dive sites in Mabini, Tingloy, Bauan, Nasugbu, Calatagan, and San Juan.
