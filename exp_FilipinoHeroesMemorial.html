Filipino Heroes Memorial

The Filipino Heroes Memorial is a 6,000-square-meter complex located on the island's southern tip. On August 28, 1992, President Fidel V. Ramos inaugurated it. It chronicles the following revolutionary moments from Philippine history: Battle of Mactan, Datu Sirongan and Sultan Kudarat in Mindanao, Bankao's Apostasy in Leyte, Sumuroy Rebellion, Andres Malong of The Murals Pangansinan, Dagohoy Revolt, Palaris Revolt in Pangasinan, Diego and Gabriela Silang in Ilocos, Hermano Pule Revolt, Philippine Revolution. The Filipino Heroes Memorial, which was built in 1987, is one of Corregidor's newest attractions, honoring the bravery of Filipino heroes in their quest for Philippine sovereignty. It contains 14 paintings depicting various moments from Philippine history, ranging from the Battle of Mactan in 1521 to the first People Power in 1986. Francisco Manosa planned the structure, and Manuel Casal sculpted the paintings and statue of a Filipino guerrilla.
