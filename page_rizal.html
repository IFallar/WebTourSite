RIZAL

GENERAL INFORMATION

The Province of (Tagalog: Lalawigan ng Rizal) is a province in the Philippines that is located in the Calabarzon region of Luzon. The city of Antipolo serves as its capital. It is located approximately east of Manila. The province is named after José Rizal, one of the Philippines’ most famous national heroes. Rizal is bounded on the west by Metro Manila, on the north by Bulacan, on the east by Quezon, and on the southeast by Laguna. The province is also located on the northern shores of Laguna de Bay, the country’s largest lake. Rizal is a mountainous province located on the western slopes of the Sierra Madre mountain range in southern Mexico.

Pasig served as the country’s capital until 1975, when it was absorbed into the newly formed National Capital Region. Antipolo has had a provincial capitol since 2009, making it the administrative center. President Rodrigo Duterte signed Republic Act No. 11475 on June 19, 2020, designating Antipolo as Rizal’s capital. This province is included in the Greater Manila Area.

Casa Santa Museum

The museum includes Casa Santa Houses, which are internationally recognized for their collections of Santa Claus figurines. With over 3,700 figures, it is currently one of the world’s most extensive private collections of Santa Claus items. The figurines and dolls can be interacted with by visitors.

Hinulugang Taktak

Hinulugang Taktak is a waterfall in the Philippine province of Rizal on the island of Luzon. The waterfall area has been designated a National Park by the Department of Environment and Natural Resources, and it is one of the two most popular tourist attractions in Antipolo, Rizal’s capital, along with the Antipolo Cathedral.

Masungi Georeserve

The Masungi Georeserve, located only an hour and a half from Metro Manila, is a conservation area and a rustic rock garden nestled in the Rizal rainforests. Masungi gets its name from the word “masungki,” which translates to “spiked” – an apt description for the sprawling limestone landscape that lies within.

Pamitinan Cave

During the April Holy Week of 1895, a year before the outbreak of the Philippine Revolution, the first in Asia, Andres Bonifacio and seven katipuneros made their pilgrimage and covenant for freedom at Pamitinan Cave. It is also known as the Bernando Carpio Cave. It is simply a plethora of caves with spectacular rock formations that form the majestic limestone crags of the Montalban Gorge. It has been designated as a National Geological Monument.

Avilon Zoo

Avilon Zoo is the Philippines' largest zoological institution, sprawling over 7.5 hectares of land and home to over 3000 exotic wildlife specimens. The Rizal Zoo also houses over 500 different species of birds, marine animals, invertebrates, and other animals, all of which you can expect to interact with during your visit through feeding and entertainment activities.
