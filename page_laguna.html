Laguna

GENERAL INFORMATION

Laguna is a province in the Philippines located in the Calabarzón region of Luzon. It is officially known as the Province of Laguna (Tagalog: Lalawigan ng Laguna; Spanish: Provincia de La Laguna). The province’s capital is Santa Cruz, and it is located southeast of Metro Manila, south of Rizal, west of Quezon, north of Batangas, and east of Cavite. Laguna hugs the southern shores of Laguna de Bay, the country’s largest lake. The province’s total population is 3,382,193 as of the 2020 census. It is also the country’s seventh wealthiest province. Laguna is well-known for being the birthplace of José Rizal, the country’s de facto national hero.

Pagsanjan Falls, the University of the Philippines Los Baños and the University of the Philippines Open University in Los Baños, the hot spring resorts of Calamba on the slopes of Mount Makiling, Pila historic town plaza, Taytay Falls in Majayjay, the wood carvings and papier-mâché created by the people of Paeté, the annual Sampaguita Festival in San Pedro, the turumba of Pakil, the Laguna is a city in the Greater Manila Area.

Enchanted Kingdom

Enchanted Kingdom (abbreviated as EK) is a Philippine theme park. It can be found in Santa Rosa, Laguna. It covers an area of 25 hectares (62 acres). Enchanted Kingdom Inc. manages and operates the park.

Pagsanjan Falls

Magdapio Falls was the original name for Pagsanjan Falls or Cavinti Falls. After an American missionary discovered this three-drop waterfall in 1902, it became one of the earliest attractions. He named it Pagsanjan Falls, which comes from the Filipino word Pinagsangahan, which means “where it branches.” It is one of the most famous waterfalls in the Philippines and near Manila and you might want to visit this one.

Carilaya Lake

Lake Caliraya is a man-made lake situated in the municipalities of Lumban, Cavinti, and Kalayaan in Laguna province, Philippines. Created in 1939, the lake has developed as a popular spot for water sports and outdoor recreation including fishing.

Cagbalete Island

Cagbalete Island is one of Quezon Province's crown jewels. It is a small island paradise in the province surrounded by cream sand that exposes significantly during low tide. The 1,640-hectare privately owned island is home to a diverse ecosystem. The island is a complete paradise for adventurers and nature lovers, with a unique variety of birds, rich marine life, and an abundance of unique plant species. Cagbalete's beach, like Jomalig Island in Northern Quezon, is dotted with Agoho trees, a family of pine trees.

Rizal Shrine

This is Dapitan City’s most important historical landmark. It refers to Dr. Jose P. Rizal’s approximately 16-hectare estate in Barangay Talisay, which he purchased with his lottery winnings. The landmark was intended to be a place of punishment, but instead it has become an icon of inspiration for many generations who come to see the national hero’s ingenuity during his days. The shrine contains a collection of structures that retell the stories of former Dapitans as well as the passion of an extraordinary man who lived as an exile with a purpose—to teach and serve the Dapitanons. The stories told there, the poems written, and the insights gained are the legacy of his life.
