QUEZON

GENERAL INFORMATION

Quezon is a Philippine province in the CALABARZON Region, located in the south of the island of Luzon. Lucena is its capital. Quezon province was named after former President Manuel L. Quezon, who was born in Baler, which was part of Tayabas at the time. Quezon is well-known for its coconut-based cuisines, including lambanog, longganisang Lucban, and pansit habhab.

The Quezon province in the Calabarzon region is known for its quaint islands and rugged mountains, as well as its coconut plantations and rice farms. Serene beaches and forests, as well as modern amenities and an exciting nightlife, draw many nature enthusiasts. A day trip to the mystical Mount Banahaw – an active three-peaked volcano that is considered a “holy” mountain and is popular with hikers – can be organized for groups. The day can be spent visiting streams, hot springs, and caves, while team-building activities such as short mountain hikes can be included.

Regina Rica

Regina RICA is an acronym that stands for Regina Rosarii Institute for Contemplation in Asia. Former Dominican Sisters of the Most Holy Rosary founded the congregation behind this 13.5-hectare pilgrimage site in 2005. The magnificent image of Our Lady of the Most Holy Rosary stands atop the hill. It’s a 71-foot-tall image of the Virgin Mary by artist and sculptor Jojo Barcena Jr. It is undeniably a work of art, and pilgrims can marvel at it, but it is not your typical massive Marian image. This massive sculpture is actually habitable, and visitors are encouraged to go inside for spiritual enlightenment and meditation.

Malinta Tunnel

The Malinta Tunnel, one of the most atmospheric features of Corregidor Island’s fortifications, was built by American forces in 1932. It grows 820 feet (250 meters) into a hill, with additional branches branching off. Before the American withdrawal from the Philippines in 1942, a portion of it served as General Douglas MacArthur’s headquarters.

Kamay ni Hesus

Kamay ni Hesus is a popular tourist destination in Quezon. The church is claimed to be a healing church, which means devotees believe that healing prayers come true here in Kamay ni Hesus.
