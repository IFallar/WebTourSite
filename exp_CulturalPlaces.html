Cultural Places

Historical events that happened can be found in some places here in CALABARZON. Visiting them may be a good idea for some people who loves studying about history of the Philippines. Some things and houses of some heroes can also be found in these provinces. Example of this is the Antipolo Cathedral. This is a 17th century Roman Catholic wooden image of the Blessed Virgin Mary, which is popular in the Philippines. Antipolo Cathedral in the Sierra Madre highlands east of Metro Manila houses the image, a Black Madonna representing the Immaculate Conception. The statue is one of the most revered images of the Blessed Virgin Mary in the Philippines, attracting admirers since the mid-nineteenth century and being cited in José Rizal's writings.


Reviews:


“The Antipolo Cathedral, which is the shrine for the Our Lady of Peace and Good Voyage, is the most popular church for travellers. It is believed that the travels of those that visit here will be both fruitful and safe. The church itself is grand and majestic. Even after recent renovations, the solemnity of the cathedral is kept intact. Every Holy Week, devotees from all over the metro trek towards this beautiful shrine as a form of their sacrifice to the Blessed Virgin Mary and to Jesus Christ. I pass by Marcos Highway going home every day. During holy week I see these devotees walking in groups and headed up the mountain where the church is situated. They bring with them backpack-worth of "baon" and different ornaments of the Sto. Niño. It is a very humbling sight to see.”

“Antipolo city is popular for being a pilgrimage site. It prides itself as the "Pilgrimage Capital of the Philippines". The Marian image of the Our Lady of Peace and Good Voyage or the Virgin of Antipolo, which was brought in from Mexico in 1626, and enshrined in the Antipolo Cathedral has a continuous following among Filipino Catholics since the Spanish colonial era. It’s a beautiful round shape with colorful windows has great aquatic. You can bless your religious items or car right after mass outside the church in a special designated area.”
