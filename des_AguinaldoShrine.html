AGUINALDO SHRINE

The Aguinaldo Mansion, also known as the House of History, is a focal point in the unfolding of Philippine history. The original structure of the house, built in 1845, was made of nipa and thatch. Four years later, it was rebuilt with a variety of Philippine hardwood. By the 1920s, major renovations had resulted in the construction of the tower and the house’s symbolic balcony. During this time, the house was also refurbished with architectural details of nationalistic themes, masonic symbols, and the art nouveau and art deco artistic styles popular in the early twentieth century. The president’s residence was given to the Filipino people in 1963, a year before he died. The house was placed under construction. The house was given to the National Museum of the Philippines in 1964 and was designated a national shrine by Republic Act No 4039. By 1972, Executive Order No. 370 had transferred the shrine to the National Historical Commission for care and maintenance.
