GOCO ANCESTRAL HOUSE

Juan Cabrera Goco and Lorenza Deomampo, husband and wife, erected the Goco House in 1876. During the Spanish occupation, the master of the house worked as a treasurer for the revolutionary forces in Taal, earning the trust of the locals. After Taal was designated a National Heritage Town by the National Historical Institute in 1999, the house was repaired. This ancestral home belongs to one of Taal, Batangas' most prominent families. The home is of the 19th century Bahay na Bato, but its contents span three centuries due to the memorabilia of Ambassador Raul and his wife Marietta Goco, both prominent officials. Pio, their son, brings the stories of Taal, its history, and the mansion to life. The tour to the residence includes a feast of typical Taal cuisine. There is also a guided tour of two museums and two cathedrals, as well as a halo halo treat.
