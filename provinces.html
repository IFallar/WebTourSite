CAVITE

Cavite is a city in the Philippines' southern Luzon region. Cavite is located on a peninsula on the southern shore of Manila Bay and serves as a residential hub for commuters to Manila, which is located to the northeast. The Cavite Mutiny, a brief and unsuccessful uprising of Filipino soldiers and workers against Spanish rule, took place in the city in 1872. The city was an important base for Emilio Aguinaldo, the insurgent leader against Spain, in 1896–1897.

LAGUNA

Laguna is a province in the Philippines located in the Calabarzón region of Luzon. It is officially known as the Province of Laguna (Tagalog: Lalawigan ng Laguna; Spanish: Provincia de La Laguna). The province’s capital is Santa Cruz, and it is located southeast of Metro Manila, south of Rizal, west of Quezon, north of Batangas, and east of Cavite. Laguna hugs the southern shores of Laguna de Bay, the country’s largest lake. The province’s total population is 3,382,193 as of the 2020 census. It is also the country’s seventh wealthiest province. Laguna is well-known for being the birthplace of José Rizal, the country’s de facto national hero.

BATANGAS

Enjoy a slice of paradise in the seaside towns and villages of Batangas. Dive into the depths of Anilao, Batangas’ underwater Eden. Swim and dive in the tropical waters of Verde Island Passage, which has been described as the world’s center of marine biodiversity. Play on Laiya Aplaya, San Juan’s famous stretch of white sand beach, and explore the numerous offshore islets. Taal Heritage Village transports you back in time with its well-preserved ancestral houses. Visit the many centuries-old churches, each of which is architecturally stunning. Try the local Batangas coffee and watch the Balisong-making process. Batangas, a multifaceted beauty, never ceases to captivate everyone with its many charms and wonderful surprises.

RIZAL

The Province of (Tagalog: Lalawigan ng Rizal) is a province in the Philippines that is located in the Calabarzon region of Luzon. The city of Antipolo serves as its capital. It is located approximately east of Manila. The province is named after José Rizal, one of the Philippines’ most famous national heroes. Rizal is bounded on the west by Metro Manila, on the north by Bulacan, on the east by Quezon, and on the southeast by Laguna. The province is also located on the northern shores of Laguna de Bay, the country’s largest lake. Rizal is a mountainous province located on the western slopes of the Sierra Madre mountain range in southern Mexico.

QUEZON

Quezon is a Philippine province in the CALABARZON Region, located in the south of the island of Luzon. Lucena is its capital. Quezon province was named after former President Manuel L. Quezon, who was born in Baler, which was part of Tayabas at the time. Quezon is well-known for its coconut-based cuisines, including lambanog, longganisang Lucban, and pansit habhab.
