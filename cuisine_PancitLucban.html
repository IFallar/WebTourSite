Pancit Lucban

Pancit habhab, also known as pancit Lucban, is a traditional Filipino pancit from the Quezon province. Dried flour noodles (miki Lucban), pork belly slices, beef stock, shrimp, pig liver, bok choy, peas, carrots, onions, garlic, soy sauce, oil, and black pepper are common ingredients. The ingredients are sautéed and stir-fried until tender, and the dish is served on a banana leaf with shrimp and vegetables on top. On the side, lime wedges are commonly served. Pancit habhab is eaten without utensils – pick up a banana leaf and eat the noodle dish like a sandwich, but don’t eat the banana leaf wrapper.
