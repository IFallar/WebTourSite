Mile Long Barracks

With a length of 1,520 feet, the Mile-Long Barracks is considered to be the "world's longest military barracks." It is close to the Pacific War Memorial and Cine Corregidor. This structure, also known as the Topside barracks, is located on the island's topside. The barracks were not a mile long (they were just a third of a mile long), but it was given that name because troops used to walk the length of the edifice from the third floor down, covering a total of 5,280 feet, which is equivalent to a mile. The three-story tower housed American officers and enlisted men assigned to the post. This building was also claimed to house General Douglas MacArthur's office/headquarters. It was once a hurricane-proof structure, but all that remains today is the building's skeleton — the result of the war, which badly devastated numerous structures on the island.
