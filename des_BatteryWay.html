BATTERY WAY

Battery Way is outfitted with four 12-inch mortar carriages capable of launching highly explosive shells up to 8.3 miles (13.35 kilometers) in any direction. To load and fire the shells, each mortar required at least 14 men. The mortars could be lifted to a trajectory ranging from 45 to 70 degrees, making them ideal for targeting enemies positioned on higher ground in Bataan. The battery was named after 4th U.S. Army Second Lt. Henry Way. Artilleryman who died in the Philippines in 1900.
